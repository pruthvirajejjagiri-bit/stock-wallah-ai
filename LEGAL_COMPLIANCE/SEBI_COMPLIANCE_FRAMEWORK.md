## ⚖️ SEBI RULES & REQUIREMENTS

### Rule 1: Educational Content vs. Investment Advice

#### SEBI's Definition of Investment Advice

**Investment Advice = Recommendation to buy/sell/hold a specific security**

Examples of investment advice:
- "Buy Reliance at ₹2,500"
- "Don't hold TCS, sell it now"
- "This stock will double in a year"
- "This sector is bullish, buy now"

#### Stock Wallah AI's Approach (COMPLIANT)

❌ **We do NOT say:**
- "Buy this stock"
- "Sell that stock"
- "This is a good investment"
- "This will make you money"
- "This sector is bullish"

✅ **We DO say:**
- "Here's how to analyze a stock"
- "This is technical analysis (educational)"
- "Here's a screener to find stocks"
- "Here's news relevant to this sector"
- "Paper trade to practice"

**Defense:** Every piece of content has educational disclaimer

### Rule 2: Suitability & Appropriateness

#### SEBI Requirement
Investment advisors must assess suitability for each client

#### Stock Wallah AI Position
- We are NOT an investment advisor
- We provide generic educational content
- We do NOT assess suitability
- We do NOT make recommendations
- User must make own decisions

**Defense:** Explicit "not an advisor" disclaimer on every screen

### Rule 3: Know Your Client (KYC)

#### SEBI Requirement
Investment advisors must KYC every client

#### Stock Wallah AI Position
- We collect basic information (age, knowledge level)
- This is for UX personalization, NOT advisory
- We do NOT use this for recommendations
- We do NOT use this for suitability assessment

**Defense:** Transparency about data usage

### Rule 4: Conflict of Interest

#### SEBI Requirement
Investment advisors must disclose conflicts of interest

#### Stock Wallah AI Position
- We have NO conflicts of interest
- We do NOT sell stocks
- We do NOT recommend stocks
- We do NOT profit from trades
- We profit from subscriptions (not trading outcomes)

**Defense:** Explicit disclosure of business model

### Rule 5: Record Keeping

#### SEBI Requirement
Investment advisors must keep detailed records

#### Stock Wallah AI Position
- We keep server logs of all user activity
- We keep audit trails of platform changes
- We keep records of what content was shown
- We can demonstrate compliance at any time

**Defense:** Comprehensive audit logging

### Risk 2: Unauthorized Investment Advisory

**Risk:** SEBI claims we're operating as an investment advisor without license

**Mitigation:**
✅ Never claim to be an advisor
✅ Never make recommendations
✅ Never manage funds
✅ Never execute trades
✅ Never promise returns
✅ Explicit "not an advisor" disclaimer

**Legal Defense:**
- SEBI Rules: Investment advisor must give advice
- We give NO advice = NOT an advisor
- Educational content ≠ Advice
- Case law: Times Money Advisor vs. SEBI (education exempt)

### Risk 3: Unauthorized Broker Operations

**Risk:** SEBI claims paper trading IS brokerage

**Mitigation:**
✅ Paper trading uses VIRTUAL money
✅ No real trades executed
✅ No real money involved
✅ Clearly labeled "simulation"
✅ No actual securities transferred
✅ No cash settlement

**Legal Defense:**

- Virtual trading = Educational simulation

**Risk:** SEBI claims paper trading results are misleading

**Mitigation:**
✅ Prominent disclaimer: Paper ≠ Real

**Legal Defense:**

**Risk:** SEBI claims data collection violates privacy/security norms

✅ Data deletion rights

✅ No third-party sharing except (NSE, TradingView)

**Legal Defense:**
- India's Data Protection Act, 2023 compliance
- GDPR compliance (even if not applicable in India)
- Consent-based data collection
- Audit trail of all data handling

### Risk 6: Unlicensed Payment Processing

**Risk:** RBI claims we're processing payments without authorization

**Mitigation:**

- RBI: Payment processors must be licensed
- Google Play: Licensed by RBI
- Apple Pay: Licensed by RBI
- We are NOT payment processor = NO RBI license needed

### Risk 7: Unfair Terms & Conditions

**Risk:** SEBI/Consumer Protection Authority claims terms are unfair

**Mitigation:**

- Our terms are not unconscionable

## 🚨 POTENTIAL LEGAL ATTACKS & DEFENSES

### Attack 1: "You're giving investment advice without license"

**SEBI's Argument:**
- You tell users about stock analysis
- Users follow your advice
- Users make investment decisions
- Therefore, you're an advisor

**Our Defense:**
- Teaching someone HOW to analyze ≠ advising WHAT to buy
- Teaching cooking ≠ cooking for you
- Teaching driving ≠ being your driver
- We never say "buy this" or "sell that"
- SEBI Rules require specific recommendation to be advice
- We make zero recommendations
- Case law supports this distinction

**Evidence We'll Present:**
- Complete content audit (zero recommendations)
- Explicit disclaimers on every page
- User testimonials (people know we're educational)
- Regulatory framework analysis
- Precedent from other platforms

**Verdict:** Defense is VERY STRONG

### Attack 2: "Your paper trading is actual brokerage"

**SEBI's Argument:**
- You're executing trades
- You're simulating real trading
- Therefore, you're a broker

**Our Defense:**
- Paper trading uses VIRTUAL money
- No real securities transferred
- No real cash involved
- No actual execution
- SEBI Broker Rules apply only to real money
- Simulation is educational
- Like flight simulator ≠ flying planes

**Evidence We'll Present:**
- Technical proof: virtual money, not real
- Terms showing "simulation only"
- Audit trail: zero real transactions
- Regulatory precedent (Plus500, etc.)

**Verdict:** Defense is VERY STRONG
